A carbon footprint below 1000 kilograms of CO2 per person per year is considered environmentally sustainable.
A footprint between 1000 and 3000 kilograms of CO2 per person per year is considered moderate.
A footprint above 3000 kilograms of CO2 per person per year is considered high and environmentally unsustainable.
Green buildings aim to reduce energy consumption by at least 40 percent compared to conventional buildings.
Net zero buildings produce as much renewable energy as they consume.
Households that reduce their emissions by at least 20 percent are considered climate responsible.
Organizations should aim to reduce carbon emissions by 30 percent within five years.
Carbon intensity measures emissions per unit of economic output and lower values indicate better sustainability performance.
Sustainable cities aim to minimize emissions while maintaining quality of life for citizens.
Low carbon lifestyles focus on reducing energy transport food and waste related emissions.
Climate responsible businesses track monitor and continuously reduce their carbon footprint.
Achieving carbon neutrality requires balancing emissions with renewable energy and offsets.
