Electricity generated in India emits approximately 0.82 kilograms of carbon dioxide (CO2) per kilowatt-hour (kWh) due to heavy dependence on coal-based power plants.
Solar electricity produces less than 0.05 kilograms of CO2 per kWh, mainly from manufacturing and installation of solar panels.
Wind energy produces around 0.02 kilograms of CO2 per kWh, making it one of the cleanest energy sources.
Petrol combustion releases approximately 2.31 kilograms of CO2 per liter burned in vehicles.
Diesel fuel emits around 2.68 kilograms of CO2 per liter when burned in engines.
Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) produces about 2.98 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram used for cooking.
Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) emits around 2.75 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram consumed.
Coal produces about 2.42 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram when burned for energy.
One kilometer of travel by a petrol car emits about 0.21 kilograms of CO2 per passenger.
One kilometer of travel by a diesel car emits approximately 0.18 kilograms of CO2 per passenger.
Bus travel produces about 0.05 kilograms of CO2 per passenger-kilometer.
Train travel produces around 0.01 kilograms of CO2 per passenger-kilometer, making it one of the cleanest transport options.
Domestic air travel produces approximately 0.15 kilograms of CO2 per passenger-kilometer.
A vegetarian diet generates roughly 2 kilograms of CO2 per person per day.
A mixed or non-vegetarian diet generates approximately 5 kilograms of CO2 per person per day due to livestock emissions and feed production.
Plastic production and disposal emits around 6 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram of plastic.
Paper production emits approximately 1.5 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram of paper.
Electronic waste (e-waste) produces up to 20 kilograms of CO2 per kilogram because of mining, smelting, and manufacturing processes.
Water supply and wastewater treatment emits approximately 0.34 kilograms of CO2 per cubic meter of water processed.
